CONCLUSION¶

In the course of our analysis, it is evident that a significant portion, over 60%, of our revenue is attributed to customers who have been with us for 0-4 years. In contrast, the 5-10 years customer segment contributes a more modest 27.8% to the total revenue, with the remaining customer base accounting for approximately 33% of our overall revenue.

The data strongly supports the conclusion that new customers are engaging positively with our offerings, showcasing a promising trend in customer acquisition. Looking ahead, it is recommended that we redirect our focus towards our longstanding customers in the upcoming quarter. This strategic shift aligns with our company values, emphasizing the importance of nurturing enduring relationships with our customer base.

Furthermore, our analysis indicates that a direct call to customers is the least effective method in terms of both time and revenue. With a call duration of approximately 30 minutes and the associated revenue being the lowest, it is proposed that we consider a modified approach. Specifically, replacing the initial contact method with an email and subsequently following up with a call after a week could prove to be a more effective strategy. This approach not only allows customers to familiarize themselves with our offerings but also provides them with the opportunity to identify their needs. By incorporating a subsequent call, we can address customer queries, enhance satisfaction, and ultimately boost revenue, particularly from our more established customer base.
